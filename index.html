IPPO: Adaptive Prompt Constraints for Goal Misgeneralization

UMass Amherst | CS 690S: AI Alignment | Spring 2026 | Submitted to COLM 2026

Studied Interleaved Prompt-Policy Optimization (IPPO) to mitigate reward hacking and goal misgeneralization during LLM RL post-training.

Final report for CS 690S: AI Alignment (co-authored with Abhishek Mishra, Arihant Barjatya, and Armaan Sandhu). We investigated whether adaptive prompt-level constraints during RL post-training can counter shortcuts that emerge as model weights change. This work has been submitted to the Conference on Language Modeling (COLM 2026).

  • Built two synthetic GSM8K multiple-choice datasets: one with positional answer bias and one with spilled-reasoning demonstration copying, to study goal misgeneralization in a controlled setting.
  • Designed a three-stage curriculum-hacking pipeline that reliably collapses Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct onto a near-pure shortcut policy while preserving format compliance, creating a harder substrate for intervention than vanilla shortcut induction.
  • Evaluated twelve intervention conditions spanning static prompts, adaptive prompt updates, reward shaping, and combined prompt + reward shaping (blind, non-blind, and oracle variants).
  • Found that prompt-only interventions cannot move a locked answer channel, but combined adaptive prompt + reward shaping consistently reduces shortcut behavior, reducing A-rate from ~1.0 to ~0.64 and improving accuracy from ~0.26 to ~0.32 under full failure-mode disclosure.

Enhancing Legal LLMs through Hybrid Retrieval, Metadata-Enriched RAG Pipelines and Direct Preference Optimization

UMass Amherst | Fall 2025

Research project addressing hallucinations in legal LLMs through improved retrieval and Direct Preference Optimization.

  • Developed metadata-enriched RAG pipelines that inject document metadata (jurisdiction, summaries) into chunks, reducing Document Retrieval Mismatch (DRM) and significantly improving span recall across legal datasets (PrivacyQA, MAUD, Australian Legal QA).
  • Implemented Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) on LLaMA 3.2 (1B) to address decoder-level hallucinations.
  • Achieved end-to-end improvements in legal document processing by combining hybrid Dense+BM25 retrieval with DPO training, demonstrating significant factual accuracy gains and selective refusal behavior in privacy policies, M&A contracts, and court judgments.
